About

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Department of Biostatistics and Bioinformatics, Emory University under supervision of Dr. Limin Peng. Previously, I obtained my Ph.D. in Statistics from the University of Pittsburgh under the supervision of Dr. Lucas Mentch. I earned my M.Phil. in Mathematics from The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology, advised by Dr. Man-yu Wong and a B.S. in Mathematics from the same institution.

My research interests lie broadly in machine learning, quantile regression, survival analysis and tensor regression. My work involves investigating the mechanism at work in methods like random forests that provide such strong, robust performance and leveraging such insights to further improve model performance and to develop highly accurate, robust algorithms to handle diverse types of data emerging from new technologies and scientific needs. Moreover, I seek to establish statistical properties of statistical learning methods to gain insights of their predictions and develop feature importance measure to aid in interpretability.
