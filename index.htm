About

 

I’m a fifth year Ph.D. student in Statistics at the University of Pittsburgh, advised by Dr. Lucas Mentch. Prior to joining Pitt, I graduated with a Master of Philosophy in Mathematics from the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology advised by Dr. Man-yu Wong in 2016. I received a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics from the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology in 2010. My research interests are generally about mechanisms behind statistical learning methods. I have been working with Dr. Lucas Mentch on understanding the role of the unique randomization in random forests from the perspective of regularization and exploring other means of regularization on ensembles of trees to produce similarly competitive procedures.
